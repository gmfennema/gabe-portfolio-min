How to Publish a Google Sheets Add-on

Post
How to Publish a Google Sheets Add-on
Published
June 4, 2022

Preface

I prefer to put the “recipe” first. Here’s what you’re signing up for before you begin the (unnecessarily frustrating) process of publishing a Sheets add‑on.

Details

  • Amount of Work: 4–8 hours
  • Approval Timeframe: 1–3 weeks
  • Frustration Level: High

Ingredients

  • Website with custom domain
  • Apps Script project
  • Promotional images
  • Google Cloud Platform account (free tier)

Part 1 – Website Shenanigans

Google Search Console

Register your site in Google Search Console. Depending on your host this can be a bit tricky, but it shouldn’t take long.

Create a Product Page

Make a clear, accessible product page describing what your add‑on does.

  • Functionality: Thoroughly describe how your add‑on enhances user workflows. Be accurate and inclusive.
  • User Data Disclaimer: Explain in plain English what data you collect and why. For most simple add‑ons: only what’s needed for core functionality plus basic analytics.
  • Privacy Policy: Link to a policy that clearly describes how you access, use, store, and share user data (see Google’s Limited Use Requirements).
  • Terms of Service: Keep it simple—just ensure it exists and is linked.
  • Avoid pitfalls: Don’t use the word “Google” in names; avoid special characters; include any consent‑screen image on your page; don’t require login to view.

Part 2 – Promotional Images

Canva is Your Friend

You likely don’t need the Adobe suite. Canva’s free tier is more than enough.

Required Sizes

  • App icon: 32×32 (required)
  • App icon: 48×48
  • App icon: 96×96
  • App icon: 128×128 (required)
  • Application card banner (required)
  • Screenshots: at least 3

Part 3 – YouTube OAuth Video

What to Record

  1. Remove your app’s permissions from your Google account: myaccount.google.com/permissions.
  2. Start a screen recording; install the add‑on and go through the “Unverified” OAuth screen.
  3. On the “Choose Account” page, highlight the client ID in the URL so the reviewer can see it.
  4. After verifying, briefly demonstrate the core functionality.
  5. Upload the video as unlisted and include the URL in your OAuth application.

Part 4 – The Application

First Things First

In Google Cloud Console, create a project. You can stay within the free tier.

OAuth Consent Screen

  1. Search for and enable the “Google Sheets API”.
  2. Go to Credentials → Create credentials → OAuth client ID → OAuth consent screen.
  3. Fill out the forms and select the appropriate OAuth scopes.
  4. Provide your YouTube video URL in the designated field.

Wait for review. Responses can range from vague rejection to the “OAuth Granted” email. Typical turnaround: 24 hours to 5 days.

Store Listing

  1. Enable the Google Workspace Marketplace SDK.
  2. Complete the listing with links and images from Parts 1 & 2.
  3. Get your Project ID from the Apps Script editor.
  4. Ensure the OAuth scopes match what you submitted for OAuth verification.
  5. Submit and wait 2–7 days for review.
