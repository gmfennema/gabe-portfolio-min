From Analytics to Content

I started out with spreadsheets, charts, and SQL—tracking KPIs, analyzing trends, and providing insights. It was valuable work, but I felt removed from the action. Joining a small startup gave me the chance to move to the front lines of content marketing, and I dove in.

After months of podcasts, blogs, Twitter threads, and trial-and-error, this is the snapshot of how I approach content marketing.

What is Content Marketing?

Content marketing creates and distributes valuable and relevant content for a clearly defined audience to move them toward an end goal.

Three ideas drive this approach.

Value Creation

No one cares about your company by default. People only engage when they get something out of it. They are the hero; you are the guide. Treat your content like a free product that helps them reach their goals.

Your customer is the hero not your brand.

— Donald Miller

Content Relevance

Value alone isn’t enough—it must be the right value for a specific audience. Example: YNAB’s YouTube channel focuses on budgeting because they sell budgeting software. Chasing broad entertainment would bring the wrong views.

Goal Orientation

Begin with the end in mind. Content should help the audience realize a need for your product and give a clear next step. Ahrefs’ technical SEO guides teach the topic while demonstrating how easy it is inside their platform.

How to Create a Content Strategy

Strategy is how you prioritize. A solid plan aligns goals, capacity, and audience demand.

What are our goals?

  • What outcome or action do we want?
  • How will we measure it?

What can we do?

  • What do we have capacity for? Is it sustainable?
  • Do we have budget for contractors or an agency?

What our audience wants/needs

  • Who is the audience? Do we need personas?
  • How will we uncover their wants and needs?

4 Types of Content Marketing

There are many formats beyond these—ebooks, whitepapers, case studies—but these four illustrate the principles well.

Educational Guides

Mode Analytics offers free intro courses to SQL and Python.

Relevant: Attracts people learning analytics who could benefit from Mode.

Valuable: Clear, approachable lessons on marketable skills.

Goal-Oriented: Tutorials nudge users to connect data—the key activation step.

Free Tools

Clearbit provides data enrichment and showcases value via free, self-serve tools.

Relevant: Targets business leaders and operators.

Valuable: Offers real insight into business viability.

Goal-Oriented: Email gating moves users into nurturing flows toward signup.

Podcasts

HubSpot invests heavily in podcasting to educate and inspire businesses of all sizes.

Relevant: More successful businesses expand HubSpot’s potential customer base.

Valuable: A portfolio of shows balances entertainment and education.

Goal-Oriented: A long-term brand play that associates HubSpot with business success.

Video Content Marketing

AlgoExpert built a large YouTube audience by speaking directly to FAANG‑aspiring engineers.

Relevant: Videos like “Google Systems Design Interview with Ex‑Googler” reach precisely the target audience.

Valuable: Insider perspectives and collaborations provide actionable guidance; engagement reflects strong perceived value.

Goal-Oriented: Clear calls to try AlgoExpert connect the content to the product.
